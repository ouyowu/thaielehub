# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Thaielehub serves English-speaking travelers, especially visitors from Europe, North America, Australia, and other long-haul markets, who are researching ethical elephant sanctuary experiences in Thailand. They may be staying in Chiang Mai, Bangkok, or Pattaya, and they need to quickly understand which departure area fits their trip, what is included, whether pickup works for their hotel, and how to book without a long conversation.

## Product Purpose

The site presents ethical elephant sanctuary tours and turns research intent into a fast booking decision. Success means a visitor can choose the right area, understand the itinerary and pickup rules, trust the welfare standards, and complete a booking in about one minute.

## Brand Personality

Calm, field-tested, and trustworthy. The site should feel like a premium travel operator with real sanctuary access: image-led, confident, warm, and practical. It should avoid looking like a generic blue Shopify store, a cheap tour aggregator, or a wildlife theme park.

## Anti-references

Avoid template-like ecommerce pages that foreground carts, discounts, product grids, and generic shopping language. Avoid dark-blue default Shopify styling, low-resolution imagery, repeated small thumbnail walls, Asian-face visitor imagery where the brief calls for Western visitor imagery, and any wording that exposes old brand names such as Living Green or Big Boy in customer-facing tour copy. Avoid copying another Shopify theme's code or assets directly.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with real sanctuary photography, not decoration.
2. Make departure area obvious: Chiang Mai, from Bangkok, and from Pattaya must be visually distinct.
3. Keep the booking path short: choose a tour, fill guest details, select pickup or meeting point, then pay when Stripe is ready.
4. Explain ethics clearly: no riding, no shows, no forced bathing, no misleading animal-contact claims.
5. Build for AI search and human skimming: precise titles, concrete itinerary details, strong internal links, and answer-shaped article content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG 2.1 AA contrast, readable 16px+ body text on mobile, visible form labels, clear focus states, and touch targets around 44px or larger. Motion should be subtle and should respect reduced-motion preferences. The site should remain usable on slower mobile connections with optimized, correctly cropped images.
